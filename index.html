About me

An educator who treats the classroom as a relationship, not a routine.

I'm an English Language Teaching specialist with over five years of classroom experience across government schools, private institutes and online platforms — working with learners from Grade 6 through Grade 13, as well as adult professionals preparing for academic or workplace English. My academic background blends ELT methodology with counseling psychology and psychotherapy, which shapes how I read a classroom: not just what students are getting wrong grammatically, but why they hesitate to speak at all.

How I actually teach

Teaching Portfolio

Student-centered, always

Lessons are built around what learners need to say, not just what the syllabus says they should know — vocabulary and grammar are introduced through real communicative tasks.

Confidence before correctness

Drawing on a counseling background, I prioritize psychological safety in speaking practice; accuracy improves once the fear of being wrong is removed.

Structured Sequencing

Each unit moves from controlled practice to free production, scaffolded for mixed-ability classrooms.

Time-Boxed Activities

Lesson plans balance input, practice and reflection within realistic classroom time constraints.

Clear Learning Outcomes

Every plan states an explicit "can-do" outcome learners can self-assess against.

Formative Checks

Low-stakes quizzes and peer feedback used continuously rather than relying solely on final exams.

Rubric-Based Speaking Tests

Transparent rubrics covering fluency, accuracy and confidence for oral assessment.

Exam-Aligned Writing Marking

Marking calibrated to O/L and international examination standards with clear written feedback.

Relationship-First Discipline

Behavioral issues addressed through rapport and understanding root causes, not punitive measures alone.

Routines & Predictability

Consistent lesson rhythms that reduce anxiety for learners who struggle with unpredictability.

Differentiated Grouping

Flexible pairing and grouping strategies to support both advanced and struggling learners in one room.

Original Worksheets

Custom-built grammar and vocabulary worksheets matched to each cohort's level.

Video Lesson Library

Recorded explainer videos learners can revisit outside class hours.

Exam-Pattern Practice Sets

Practice papers modeled directly on O/L and international assessment formats.

Blended Learning Tools

Combining in-class instruction with digital quizzes, recorded content and online discussion boards.

Video-Based Instruction

Self-produced video content used for flipped-classroom style lessons.

Data-Informed Adjustments

Using quiz and assignment results to identify and address common learner gaps quickly.

Continuous self-review

Every term ends with a personal review of what worked, what didn't, and what to adjust before the next cohort.

Peer & mentor feedback

Actively seeking observation and feedback from fellow educators to keep refining classroom practice.
